Ilayaraja - from Pannaipuram to Budapest

Initial years starting 1976

Ilayaraja, the Maestro, began his career as an independent Music director in 1976 with the blockbuster soundtrack of "Annakkili". The album represented a mix of rural folk music with western orchestration. It announced the arrival of authentic Tamil country music into Madras' movie screens. There was absolutely no holding back the Maestro from this point onwards. Within the next 5 years, he had established himself as the numero Uno in Tamil Film Music.

40 films a year

The 80s Monopoly

Ilayaraja had become prolific to the point of scoring over 40 films a year through the 80s. The quantity had a quality in itself. He was the first choice of all directors - experienced and freshers. He didn't disappoint anybody, even though his directors may have. Films like Thangamagan represented his standard film - an album full of blockbusters with Disco, Carnatic-classical and rural touches all adequately represented. As it turned out, no other musician has been able to match his prolificity till date. This was Ilayaraja's era through and through.

Flourish after the flood

Competition catches up in 90s, 00s

The unchallenged territory of Raja ceased to exist with the arrival of newer directors and music directors in the 90s. But Raja continued to deliver rich collections year after year for another decade. He remains active and brilliantly so. Just that the 80s era turned out to be once in a lifetime not just for him; but for Tamil film industry itself. Films like Hey Ram represent the pinnacle of Indian classical fusion with Western Orchestrations, though such hits weren't as frequent as in the 80s.
